## RISK MANAGEMENT RULES

### THE ONLY RULES THAT MATTER

```
1. NEVER risk more than 1% of capital per trade
2. ALWAYS have a stop loss BEFORE entering
3. Minimum 2:1 risk-reward ratio
4. Maximum 5 open positions at once
5. Maximum 2% daily loss, then STOP
6. Maximum 10% total drawdown, then PAUSE
```

### Position Sizing Formula

```
Position Size = (Capital × Risk%) / (Entry - Stop Loss)

Example:
- Capital: ₹5,00,000
- Risk per trade: 1% = ₹5,000
- Entry: ₹100
- Stop Loss: ₹95
- Risk per share: ₹5

Position Size = ₹5,000 / ₹5 = 1,000 shares
```

### When to STOP Trading

1. Hit daily loss limit (2%)
2. Hit drawdown limit (10%)
3. Breaking rules due to emotions
4. Market is choppy/no trend
5. You're tired or distracted

## STRATEGY PARAMETERS

### 1. Trend Following (Best for Beginners)
```
Timeframe: 15min or 1hr
Indicators: 
- EMA 20, 50, 200
- RSI (14)
- ATR (14)
- Volume SMA (20)

Entry Rules:
- Price > EMA 200 (uptrend)
- Price pulls back to EMA 20
- RSI > 50 (momentum)
- Volume > 1.5x average

Stop Loss: 2x ATR below entry
Take Profit: 3x ATR above entry (3:1 R:R)
```

### 2. Mean Reversion
```
Timeframe: 5min
Indicators:
- Bollinger Bands (20, 2)
- RSI (14)
- VWAP

Entry Rules:
- Price at lower BB + RSI < 30 = BUY
- Price at upper BB + RSI > 70 = SELL
- BB width < 8% (range-bound market)

Stop Loss: Below/above BB + ATR
Take Profit: Middle BB
```

### 3. Options Selling
```
Instrument: NIFTY or BANK NIFTY options
Timeframe: 30-45 DTE

Cash-Secured Puts:
- Sell put at support level
- Delta: 0.25-0.30
- Premium: Minimum ₹50
- When: Market is neutral to bullish

Covered Calls:
- Sell call on stocks you own
- At resistance level
- Delta: 0.25-0.30
```

## BACKTESTING RULES

### What to Test
1. Minimum 200 trades (statistical significance)
2. At least 1 year of data
3. Include slippage and fees
4. Test across different market conditions

### Metrics That Matter

| Metric | Good | Great | Exceptional |
|--------|------|-------|-------------|
| Win Rate | >50% | >55% | >60% |
| Profit Factor | >1.3 | >1.8 | >2.5 |
| Sharpe Ratio | >1.0 | >1.5 | >2.0 |
| Max Drawdown | <15% | <10% | <5% |
| Calmar Ratio | >1.5 | >2.0 | >3.0 |

### Backtesting Pitfalls
1. **Overfitting**: Perfect on historical, terrible live
2. **Look-ahead bias**: Using future data
3. **Ignoring fees**: ₹20/order adds up fast
4. **Survivorship bias**: Using only current NIFTY stocks
